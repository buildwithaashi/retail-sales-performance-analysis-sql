# 🛍️ Retail Sales Performance Analysis using SQL

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data using SQL to answer real-world business questions related to customers, products, orders, and revenue generation.

The objective of the project is to demonstrate how SQL can be used to transform raw transactional data into meaningful business insights that support decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze customer purchasing behavior.
- Calculate revenue generated by completed orders.
- Identify top revenue-generating customers and cities.
- Detect customers who have never placed an order.
- Identify products that have never been ordered.
- Categorize products based on price using SQL.
- Generate business insights from sales data.

## 🧠 Business Questions Solved

1. Display complete sales reports including customer, product, quantity and order status.
2. Calculate revenue generated by completed orders.
3. Find customers generating the highest revenue.
4. Calculate city-wise revenue.
5. Identify customers who have never placed an order.
6. Categorize products into Premium, Standard and Budget.
7. Find completed orders generating more than the average completed-order revenue.
8. Identify products that have never been ordered.

## 📊 Key Insights

- Delhi generated the highest completed-order revenue, followed by Mumbai.
- Premium-priced products contributed a significant portion of total revenue.
- The Monitor product received no orders during the analyzed period.
- Two customers (Rohan and Meera) had never placed an order.
- Revenue was concentrated among a small number of customers.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Investigate why the Monitor product received no orders before making inventory decisions.
- Explore promotional strategies in lower-performing cities such as Jaipur.
- Continue monitoring demand for premium products while evaluating opportunities to expand their sales.
- Design marketing campaigns targeting inactive customers.
